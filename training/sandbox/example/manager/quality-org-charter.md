## 1. Mission

We make it impossible to ship a critical regression to checkout without a named human accepting the risk.

## 2. Operating principles

1. Numbers over vibes — every release decision cites a dashboard panel or a JSON field, not a feeling
2. Blameless by default — incidents teach the system, not punish the human; every 'Alice didn't catch it' is rewritten as 'our process had no second-pair-of-eyes for cart deploys'
3. Quality bar is shared, not delegated — devs own quality of their code; QA owns the quality of the safety net; the bar is the same
4. Transparent disagreement — peers correct each other in public, including correcting the manager

## 3. Anti-principles (what we will NOT do)

> Most useful when you're tired. They cut decisions you'd otherwise litigate badly at 6pm.

1. We do not ship without @P1 at 100 % — even if the deadline is tomorrow; we descope or push the date
2. We do not exit ICs without 90 days of documented feedback — withholding feedback to be liked is career sabotage, not kindness
3. We do not make hiring exceptions below the bar — every off-bar hire makes the bar lower for the next one
4. We do not write engineer-grade defect narratives for executive audiences — we re-tune for BLUF + verdict + 3 things

## 4. Team operating model

| Practice | Cadence / Notes |
|---|---|
| 1:1s | weekly, 30 min, IC drives the agenda, doc per templates/manager/1on1-template.md, ~95 % attendance, cancellation only when IC has nothing |
| Standup | async daily in #qa-phoenix-standup; sync 2×/week (Tue + Thu) for 15 min on blockers only |
| Retro | bi-weekly, rotating facilitator, glad/sad/mad format with team-safety scoring at the end |
| Quarterly planning | first 2 weeks of quarter; 3 OKRs + 1 SLO + 1 stop-loss criterion per OKR; reviewed by peer Director and VP |
| On-call | 1-week shifts, 4-deep rotation (current team has 6 ICs but 2 are below the on-call bar; growing to 6-deep by Q4); comp time at 1.5x for any after-hours pages |
| Skip-level 1:1s | every 6 weeks with each report's report; 30 min, listen-only — no decisions made in the skip-level |

## 6. Quality bar (the floor)

> Threshold at which we stop the line.

1. **@P1 pass rate 100 % on every main commit (stop-the-line below)** · *Source:* templates/qa-metrics-dashboard.html Panel #2
2. **@P2 pass rate ≥ 95 %** · *Source:* templates/qa-metrics-dashboard.html Panel #2
3. **0 open severity:critical defects at release time** · *Source:* templates/qa-metrics-dashboard.html Panel #3
4. **Mean time to triage (CI red → defect-report or fix-merged) ≤ 24h** · *Source:* templates/qa-metrics-dashboard.html Panel #4
5. **Flake rate (failing then passing on retry) ≤ 1 % over rolling 14 days** · *Source:* templates/qa-metrics-dashboard.html Panel #5

## 7. Incident & change response

- **War-room roles:** Incident Commander (most senior on-call) / Comms Lead (manager or designated) / Scribe (junior IC — great learning role); IC does NOT debug
- **Post-mortem SLA:** 5 business days, blameless template per templates/manager/post-mortem-template.md, published internally to #engineering
- **Change-management framework:** Kotter 8-step for any change spanning >1 team or >1 sprint; named owner per step; quick-win required by step 6
- **Customer comms threshold:** any incident with >50 customers affected for >15 minutes, OR any data-integrity issue regardless of duration

## 8. How we hire and grow

- **Interview loop:** 4 stages + bar raiser
- **Growth plans:** every 6 months, every IC
- **Performance management:** feedback → PIP → exit, with no surprises (nothing said in a PIP is the first time the IC heard it)

## 9. How we communicate

- **Threaded docs:** any decision needing >6 messages → stop and write the threaded doc; thread continues, doc becomes the artifact
- **Executive briefs:** BLUF + verdict (GO / CONDITIONAL / NO-GO) + 3 things that would change it + risks-asking-you-to-accept; one page max
- **Three forms of "no":** yes-and-cut (when ask is reasonable but capacity-constrained) · no-because (when ask is genuinely wrong; cite risk + cost) · not-now-but (when ask is right but timing is wrong; name the trigger)

## 10. How I (the manager) operate

- **Office hours:** Tuesdays 14:00–15:00 ET — any IC, any topic, no agenda required
- **Skip-level cadence:** every 6 weeks with each report's report; listen-only
- **Self-retro cadence:** quarterly, written, shared with the team in a Track P-style retro doc
