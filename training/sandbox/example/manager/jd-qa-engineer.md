# Senior QA Automation Engineer (L5) — Phoenix QA / Checkout & Cart

> Filled-in JD for the example role used across the Phoenix QA worked example. Position opens 2026-05-01; loop runs through 2026-06-15.

## What you'll do (90-day shippable outcomes)

- **Lead the e2e suite for cart and checkout** — own pass-rate, flake rate, and CI duration SLOs (currently @P1 100 %, flake 1.8 %, runtime 28 min on 4 shards)
- **Architect testability** for the upcoming v3.0 cart redesign in collaboration with the cart product team (PR-time, not after-the-fact)
- **Mentor 1 mid-level IC** (Maya or Priya, depending on fit) through code review and 2 weekly pair-debug sessions
- **Co-own the AI eval gate rollout in CI** with Tom Liu — your slice is the regression-safety gate; Tom's is the eval infra
- **Carry on-call** in the 4-deep rotation alongside Sam, Tom, and the new hire

## Must have (5 — every bullet is a hard filter we'll test in the loop)

- 3+ years writing production test code in Playwright, Cypress, or equivalent, with **strong TypeScript**
- Experience **owning a CI pipeline** (sharding, flake triage, artifact debugging) — you've made one faster, not just used one
- Examples of **mentoring or teaching ICs** — visible PRs, internal talks, or docs you authored
- Comfort **operating in ambiguity** — when a spec doesn't exist, you write the proposal (we'll see this in the design round)
- Production **incident response experience** — you've been in a war room for a sev-1 or sev-2 in any role

## Nice to have (bonus signal — not required)

- AI-assisted testing experience (Cursor, Copilot, MCP, prompt eval frameworks like Braintrust / LangSmith / Humanloop)
- a11y or visual-regression depth (axe-core, Percy, Applitools)
- Open-source contributions to a testing framework or plugin
- Performance / load-testing background (k6, JMeter)
- Experience with the e-commerce domain (cart, checkout, payments)

## Red flags (we'll surface these in the loop and pass on them)

- "I prefer to work alone, no PRs, no reviews"
- "I don't code; I run test cases" — this is a manual-QA hire and not what we're filling
- "Tests are flaky because devs don't follow standards" — pattern of blaming the dev side rather than designing for testability
- Can't describe **one specific bug they let escape** and what they learned (everyone has one; people who claim they don't are missing self-awareness)
- Prefers to debug by adding logging rather than reading the trace — Phase 2 / 5 disciplines are not optional here
