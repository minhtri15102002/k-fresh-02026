# Phase 7 — AI-Era QA Leadership & Strategy

> Phase 6 taught you to *use* AI in your daily QA work. Phase 7 teaches you to **lead with AI** — shape teams, govern risk, test AI systems themselves, justify ROI, and become the kind of engineer 2026+ companies fight to hire.
>
> This phase is **strategic and reflective**, not framework-heavy. Expect more reading, debate, and writing — fewer locators.

## Phase outcomes

You can:

- Articulate **how AI is reshaping QA** — what disappears, what amplifies, what's brand new — and brief leadership on the change.
- Build a **personal upskilling roadmap** spanning AI, cloud, APIs, DevOps, observability, and security — and execute it with hands-on projects, not just courses.
- Design tests for **AI-powered features** that go beyond functional pass/fail — covering fairness, reliability, robustness, hallucination, and prompt injection.
- Stand up **trust & governance guardrails**: validation gates, human-in-the-loop checkpoints, monitoring, and rollback plans for AI features.
- Pitch and measure **AI-adoption ROI** in business terms (cycle time, defect-escape rate, cost per release, customer trust) — not in "lines of code generated."
- Recognize the **risks of overusing AI** (skill atrophy, hidden failures, governance gaps) and counter them with deliberate practice and review discipline.
- Step into **AI Quality Leader** roles — running governance reviews, owning reliability KPIs, and mentoring engineers through the transition.

## Phase self-check

- [ ] You can give a 10-minute talk titled **"What our QA org will look like in 18 months"** backed by examples from this codebase.
- [ ] You have a written 12-month upskilling plan with quarterly milestones and one shipped artifact per quarter.
- [ ] You have authored at least one **AI-feature test plan** (LLM, recommender, classifier, or agent) using the patterns from Module 36.
- [ ] You can explain — to a non-technical executive — what governance the team needs before turning on an AI feature in production.
- [ ] You can compute and defend an **AI ROI** number for a real workflow you've adopted (e.g. "AI-assisted POM generation cut new-feature setup from 6h → 1.5h, saving ~18 engineer-days per quarter").
